CASE TIMELINE

5 Weeks

ROLE

Interaction Designer- Research |Visual Design| Illustration| Ux writing

TOOLS

Figma | Adobe Illustrator

SUMMARY

Divercity- Diversity & Inclusion solved; which is a recruitment platform, which focuses on solving diversity and inclusion for professionals and recruiters across every sector and organisation The need to improve interaction for users was needed on empty states. I had to study all features of the app and create illustrations for each state that returns no content, for both web and mobile

CHALLENGE

Users are shown a blank screen or just a message to indicate empty states while using their solution and users are left confused, as to what is really happening, as most times they just go back or refresh the page, this was causing a serious bounce rate and uninstalling of their app. To better improve interaction for users, empty states were needed

PROCESS
  1. Discover - Competitive Analysis research
  2. Understand - Document findings | Moodboard build up
  3. Ideate - Illustrate | High Fidelity | Ux writing
  4. Review - User testing
RESEARCH

The stakeholders sent me a brief containing the brand guide, about the company, and also a list of competitors. I researched all the features that they share and took note of the empty states that are been used and most importantly the call-to-action, users are to take at each state. This competitive analysis was further sent back to the Design team to review, this was to help me outline all the features that required empty states. I navigated carefully all through the app with the UX researcher and User researcher from the team, to capture all points that required empty states illustration for both web and mobile.

Here is a snippet of the analysis and ongoing feedback for the notification empty states

UNDERSTAND

Bearing in mind insights from the research phase and the goal to make the empty states easy for users to adapt to and also what call-to-action was needed on each page, I jumped right into illustration and flow for each feature from the main touchpoint. I identified the major elements and explored ways to represent them visually.

I collaborated with the Ux researcher and User researcher, as to proper and simple words that would help convey and not misinform users on what step to take next when they land on an empty state. Also observe, for the case of their brand, the Humaaans library was used, as requested by the stakeholders, be that the brand was people-focused

TESTING

The stakeholders, and some selected recruiters, and a few users were contacted to test the new states. Users navigated the app and keen attention was given to whether users would know what next decision to take when confronted with an empty state, or if what they wished was contained in terms of information, was the need of the users. 80% of the users that tested, had no issues with what next to do or understand what was wrong earlier, the recruiters although felt they were a lot of empty state illustration and didn't seem it necessary. Those empty state illustrations were made synonymous to each other and another test session was conducted for existing users, feedback from these sessions was key to iterating the interface design into the final solution
